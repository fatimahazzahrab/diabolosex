The Origin of NAKBA



What is Nakba and How's it Started?

NAKBAR, is an Arab word for “catastrophe,” it signals a mass eviction from the 1940s that created a refugee crisis. It is the violent displacement and dispossession of the Palestinian people, along with the destruction of their society, culture, identity, political rights, and national aspirations. The term is used to describe the events that took place during the 1948 Palestine war, as well as the ongoing persecution and displacement of Palestinians by Israel throughout the Palestinian territories (the West Bank and the Gaza Strip).
Many scholars, including Israeli historian Ilan Pappé, describe the events of the Nakba 1948 as an instance of ethnic cleansing. And it was, in fact, an ethnic cleansing by-something-you-cannot-call-a-human.
